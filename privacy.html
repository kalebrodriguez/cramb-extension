Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 23, 2026

The short version.
  • Cramb has no backend and no accounts. There is no Cramb server to send your data to.
  • Your decks, cards, captured content, and review history live only on your device.
  • Your API key is stored locally and used only to talk to the AI provider you choose.
  • No analytics, no tracking, no telemetry. Cramb never "phones home."

1. Who this applies to

This policy covers the Cramb browser extension ("Cramb", "the extension"). Cramb is open-source software you run in your own browser. It is not a hosted service, and its developers do not operate any server that receives your data.

2. What Cramb stores, and where

Everything Cramb creates stays on your device:

DataWhere it's stored
Decks, cards, captured sources, and review historyYour browser's local database (IndexedDB)
Settings (chosen provider, model, Ollama endpoint)chrome.storage.local on your device
Your AI provider API keychrome.storage.local on your device

None of this is transmitted to the developers of Cramb. It is never uploaded to a Cramb server, because there isn't one.

3. Your API key

You bring your own API key. Cramb never ships, bundles, or shares a key. Your key is:

  • stored only in chrome.storage.local on your device;
  • read only by the extension's background service worker, solely to authenticate requests to the provider you selected;
  • never sent to a content script or web page, never logged, and never included in any export.

4. Content sent to AI providers

When you ask Cramb to generate flashcards, the relevant text — your highlighted selection, an article's extracted text, or a video transcript — together with the generation prompt is sent directly from your browser to the one AI provider you configured, and to no one else:

  • OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google — if you use one of their APIs with your key; or
  • nowhere off your machine, if you use a local Ollama model (requests go to localhost).

That content is handled under your chosen provider's privacy policy and your account's settings with them. Cramb does not see, intercept, or retain it. If privacy is paramount, use Ollama for fully local generation.

5. Capturing pages and videos

Cramb only reads a page's content when you explicitly capture it (via the toolbar button or the right-click "Make cards from selection" menu). To capture a YouTube video's transcript, Cramb requests it from youtube.com. Cramb does not run in the background collecting your browsing history, and it does not track the pages you visit.

6. No tracking or analytics

Cramb contains no analytics, no telemetry, no advertising, and no third-party trackers. It makes network requests only to: (a) the AI provider you configured, and (b) youtube.com when you capture a video. It does not "phone home" to any Cramb-operated endpoint, because none exists.

8. Exports

Cramb can export your library as a JSON backup or an Anki .apkg file. These files are generated locally and saved by you, to wherever you choose. Exports never contain your API key. Once a file leaves Cramb, it's under your control.

9. Data retention and deletion

Because all data is local, you are in control of it. You can delete decks and cards inside the extension, or remove everything by clearing the extension's storage or uninstalling Cramb. There is no server-side copy for us to delete, and nothing persists once you remove it from your device.

10. Children's privacy

Cramb is a general-purpose study tool and is not directed at children under 13. It collects no personal information and operates entirely on the user's device.

11. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "Last updated" date above will change and the revision history is available in the project's public Git repository. Material changes will be noted in the release notes.

12. Contact

Questions or concerns? Please open an issue on the Cramb GitHub repository.
